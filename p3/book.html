Catch-22 Book Cover

RIT - New Media Survey I

This project was from my first semester at RIT, but I was still really happy with the concept and the way that it turned out (not to mention the book I designed the cover for, Catch 22, is one of my favorite books. Because the book deals with the ridiculousness of war and the entire title of the book revolves around the concept that you'd have to be insane to fly into the certain death of their missions, and an insane person can't fly, but only a sane person would refuse to fly them- thus, no matter what, you need to fly these dangerous missions. There is a very dark humor about the book overall in addition to the childlike ridiculousness and I loved the concept of illustrating that with a child's drawing of an pilot smiling happily in his plane as a missile is seconds away from killing him.
